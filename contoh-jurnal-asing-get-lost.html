36. 1.How do you usually get to a place if you don't know the way? Explain.2.Do you think there are still some people who use paper maps to know directions? Tell us the reasons. 3. Have you ever got lost before? Explain and tell us how you could get lost. 4.If somebody get lost, what is the first thing you think they should do?​


1. If I don't know the way to a place, there are several ways I might get there:

• I might use a GPS navigation system on my phone or in my car.

• I might consult a map (either a digital map on my phone or tablet, or a paper map) to plan my route and figure out the directions.

• I might ask for directions from someone who is familiar with the area.

2. Yes, there are definitely some people who still use paper maps to know directions. Some people prefer paper maps because they are easy to read and don't require a battery or signal to use. Additionally, some people may be more comfortable with a physical map they can hold in their hands rather than looking at a screen.

3. Yes, I have gotten lost before. I have gotten lost while hiking in unfamiliar areas, as well as while driving in new cities. I've also gotten lost in airports or malls, especially when I am in a hurry or distracted. When I get lost, I try to stay calm and retrace my steps to the last place I remember being. If that doesn't work, I might ask for directions from someone who works in the area or consult a map to figure out where I am and where I need to go.

4. If someone gets lost, the first thing they should do is try to stay calm and assess their situation. They should think about where they are, where they need to go, and what resources they have available to them (such as a mobile phone or a map). They should also consider their physical and emotional state and prioritize their safety and well-being. If they are in a situation where they are lost in the wilderness or an unfamiliar area, they should try to find shelter, water, and food if possible, and then call for help if they are unable to find their way on their own.
