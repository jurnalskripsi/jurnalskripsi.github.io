1. teman2 ada yang tau , Contoh dari Semua cabang linguistik? seperti phnologi, sociolinguistics, Dll ? Linguistics Inggris


• Phonology

Phonology discusses how words can form a linguistic form.

• Phonetics

Phonetics examines how naturally brothers can be created through human organs and symbolize them in alphabetical structure.

• Linguistic sociology (sociological linguistics)

is a word of language that adheres to words that cannot be expressed from a human social context, and which perfects the analysis of language with the style of expression of people or groups.

• Morphology

Morphological studies examine higher linguistic elements, namely words. The morphology of how sound elements arranged in alphabetical order is a linguistic unit that is larger than that arranged into a new element that has meaning.

• Semantic

If morphology assesses the word, then Semantics examines the meaning. The words arranged are a form of meaning when combined with various linguistic elements (such as punctuation). Morphology also examines how words form larger linguistic units such as phrases, clauses or sentences.

• Pragmatics

In the study of Pragmatic studies, the meaning that has been determined by the arrangement of words will be studied based on each context. Language that represents the communication system of fellow humans has multiple or hidden meanings in it. In this study the meanings are learned.

• Syntax

In this study, the structure of language is profound. In classifying the language units discussed above, not only see the meaning that can be used but there are certain rules where words, phrases, clauses until the sentence must be arranged in accordance with the rules and linguistic structures that have been determined. This is where the rules, including word structure and grammar, are studied scientifically.  

• Lexicology

If syntax forms a pattern of unit combinations of language units in structure and language, then the lexicology of composing patterns of linguistic units in the study of their meaning. The Leksikologi study examines how units of language units need to be digested to get meaning.


Artinya :

• Fonologi

Fonologi membahas tentang bagaimana kata-kata yang dapat membentuk suatu bentuk kebahasaan.

• Fonetik

Fonetik mengkaji tentang bagaimana alaminya bersaudara dapat tercipta melalui organ-organ manusia dan melambangkannya dalam struktur alfabetis.

• Linguistik sosiologi (sosiologis linguistik)

adalah kata bahasa yang berpegang pada kata-kata yang tidak dapat diungkapkan dari konteks sosial manusia, dan yang menyempurnakan analisis bahasa dengan gaya pengungkapan orang atau kelompok.

• Morfologi

Studi Morfologi mengkaji elemen-elemen kebahasaan yang lebih tinggi yaitu kata. Morfologi bagaimana elemen-elemen bunyi yang disusun dalam susunan alfabetis adalah satuan satuan kebahasaan yang lebih besar dari yang tersusun menjadi sebuah elemen baru yang memiliki makna.

• Semantik

Jika morfologi mengakaji tentang kata, maka Semantik mengkaji tentang makna. Kata-kata yang tersusun merupakan rupa makna ketika dikombinasikan dengan berbagai elemen kebahasaan (seperti tanda baca). Morfologi juga mengkaji bagaimana kata itu membentuk unit-unit kebahasaan yang lebih besar lagi seperti frase, klausa atau kalimat.

• Pragmatik

Didalam kajian studi Pragmatik, makna yang sudah ditentukan oleh susunan kata tersebut akan dikaji berdasarkan masing-masing konteksnya. Bahasa yang mewakili sistem komunikasi sesama manusia memiliki makna yang ganda atau tersembunyi didalamnya. Pada kajian inilah makna-makna tersebut dipelajari.

• Sintaksis

Di dalam kajian ini, struktur-struktur bahasa secara mendalam. Dalam mengelompokkan unit-unit kebahasaan yang telah dibahas di atas, tidak hanya melihat makna yang dapat digunakan tetapi ada aturan-aturan tertentu dimana kata, frase, klausa hingga kalimat tersebut harus disusun sesuai dengan aturan dan struktur kebahasaan yang sudah ditentukan. Disinilah aturan-aturan tersebut, termasuk susunan kata dan tata bahasa (grammar), dikaji secara ilmiah.

• Leksikologi

Jika sintaksis membentuk pola kombinasi satuan satuan-satuan bahasa dalam struktur dan juga kebahasaan, maka leksikologi penyusunan pola unit-unit kebahasaan itu dalam kajian maknanya. Studi Leksikologi mengkaji tentang bagaimana satuan satuan-satuan bahasa perlu digigris untuk mendapatkan makna.

Sociolinguistics is a field of study which deals mostly with language use, particularly spoken language, but not the grammatical or phonetic structure of a language. Sociolinguists do not study a language or a linguistic variety as a closed system with no reference to speaker and social life.In linguistics we sometimes might seem to treat language as though it was nothing to do with people. Social context recognises that people use language and that language is a part of society. Social context tries to describe, and account for, the different ways that different people use language.The language that we speak influences our cultural identities and our social realities. We internalize norms and rules that help us function in our own culture but that can lead to misunderstanding when used in other cultural contexts. We can adapt to different cultural contexts by purposely changing our communication.Socio-cultural context refers to the idea that language, rather than existing in isolation, is closely linked to the culture and society in which it is used. This means when language is learnt, the socio-cultural context in which it is used needs to be taken into consideration as well.

7. Write some examples of sociolinguistics research!​


Language attitudes and stereotypes: Sociolinguists may investigate how individuals perceive and evaluate different language varieties and the social implications associated with those attitudes. For example, a study could explore the perception of accents in hiring practices or how linguistic prejudices affect social interactions.Language and identity: Sociolinguists often examine how language use reflects and shapes individual and group identities. They may study language choices among multilingual speakers and investigate how language plays a role in constructing social identities, such as gender, ethnicity, or social class.Language variation and change: Sociolinguists investigate how language varies within a community or across different communities. They may explore regional dialects, urban vernaculars, or the use of different language varieties based on social contexts. Research might involve analyzing linguistic features, such as pronunciation, vocabulary, or grammatical structures, and studying their distribution and evolution.Language and power: Sociolinguistic research often examines how language is used to exert power and control within society. This can include studying language policies, language planning, or the role of language in institutional settings, such as education, law, or media. Researchers might investigate linguistic inequalities and how language can be a tool for social, economic, or political dominance.Language and social interaction: Sociolinguists analyze the ways in which language is used in everyday communication and social interactions. They might study conversational patterns, speech acts, politeness strategies, or the negotiation of meaning in different contexts. This research can provide insights into how language reflects and shapes social relationships and social dynamics.Language and migration: Sociolinguists may focus on language use and acquisition among immigrant communities. They might explore language maintenance and shift, bilingualism, language contact phenomena, and the impact of language on the integration and identity formation of migrants and their descendants.Language and technology: With the rise of digital communication platforms and social media, sociolinguistic research has also expanded to examine how language is used and influenced by technology. This can involve studying online language practices, computer-mediated communication, text messaging, or the impact of technology on language change and variation.Language policy and planning: Sociolinguists often engage in research on language policies at national, regional, or local levels. This can include investigating language revitalization efforts, language endangerment, language rights, or language-in-education policies. Researchers may explore the sociopolitical factors that shape language policy decisions and their consequences for linguistic diversity and social inclusion.

These examples illustrate the broad range of topics within sociolinguistics, and researchers employ various methodologies, including surveys, interviews, ethnography, corpus analysis, and quantitative techniques, to investigate these areas of inquiry.

16. what the definition about style, context and register in sociolinguistics course?


 a style is a set of linguistic variants with specific social meanings. social meanings can include group membership, personal attributes, or beliefs.

verbal context refers to the text or speech surrounding an expression (word, sentence, or speech act). Verbal context influences the way an expression is understood; hence the norm of not citing people out of context.

a register is a variety of a language used for a particular purpose or in a particular social setting. For example, when speaking in a formal setting contrary to an informal setting, an English speaker may be more likely to use features of prescribed grammar—such as pronouncing words ending in -ing with avelar nasal instead of an alveolar nasal (e.g. "walking", not "walkin'"), choosing more formal words (e.g. father vs. dad, child vs. kid, etc.), and refraining from using words considered nonstandard, such as ain't.
