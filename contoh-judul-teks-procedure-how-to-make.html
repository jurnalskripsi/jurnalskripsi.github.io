4. procedure text how to make kebab


How to make kebab
*Makes four patties which are 20 centimeter (7.9 in) x 5 cm (8" x 2").
*500-700g (around 1 1/4 lb) ground/minced meat; traditionally this dish is made with lamb meat mixed with fat (preferably from the lamb tail) in proportion of 1:5; you can also use half beef, half mutton or just beef
*1 onion
*1 red sweet pepper (or substitute dried red pepper flakes for a more traditional and spicier variation)
*1/2 tbsp coriander
*1/2 tbsp cumin
*1 tbsp butter
*1 tbsp biber (Turkish red pepper paste)
*A little bit olive oil
*Salt and pepper
@Steps
1.Put the minced meat in a bowl. If you have half beef, half mutton then mix it a bit.
2.Cut the butter and the onion into small pieces and add them to the minced meat. Add also the coriander, cumin and biber.
3.Cut the sweet pepper into a few big chunks and use an immersion blender to puree the sweet pepper. If you have no immersion blender you can also cut the sweet pepper into very small pieces.
4.Add the puree to the minced meat.
5.Add salt and pepper.
6.Knead the minced meat.
7.Divide the minced meat into four equal sized, rectangle shaped kebabs.
If you are using the long skewer, you may not need to divide the meat. You would make a long patty to put on the skewer, as shown.
8.Cover the kebabs with olive oil.
Grill the kebabs in an oven, electric indoor grill or on an outdoor grill. With the oven it takes about 15 minutes, with an electric indoor grill it takes about 5 minutes.

"Panjang Mulud" is a traditional Indonesian dish made from glutinous rice cake that is commonly enjoyed during special occasions or as a snack. Here is a procedure on how to make "Panjang Mulud":

Ingredients:

2 cups of glutinous rice

2 cups of coconut milk

1 cup of brown sugar

A pinch of salt

Pandan leaves for flavor (optional)

Instructions:

1. Wash and Soak the Glutinous Rice:

Begin by washing the glutinous rice thoroughly until the water runs clear.Then, soak the rice in water for at least 2-3 hours or preferably overnight. This will help soften the rice and make it easier to cook.

2. Prepare the Steamer:

While the rice is soaking, prepare a steamer by boiling water in a large pot or a steamer apparatus. Make sure the water is boiling and producing steam.

3. Drain the Soaked Rice:

After soaking, drain the water from the glutinous rice using a fine mesh strainer.

4. Cook the Rice:

Line a steamer tray or basket with pandan leaves (if using) to add flavor. You can also use a banana leaf for this purpose.Place the drained glutinous rice onto the tray or basket.Steam the rice over high heat for about 30-40 minutes or until the rice is fully cooked and becomes translucent. You may want to check the rice occasionally and stir it to ensure even cooking.

5. Prepare the Coconut Sauce:

While the rice is cooking, prepare the coconut sauce. In a separate saucepan, combine the coconut milk, brown sugar, and a pinch of salt.Cook the mixture over low to medium heat until the sugar is completely dissolved, and the sauce becomes slightly thickened. Stir continuously to prevent burning.

6. Combine Rice and Coconut Sauce:

Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a mixing bowl.Pour the warm coconut sauce over the cooked glutinous rice.Gently stir and fold the rice into the sauce until it is well-coated and has absorbed the sweet coconut flavor.

7. Serve Panjang Mulud:

"Panjang Mulud" can be served warm or at room temperature.It can be molded into a desired shape, cut into squares, or served as is.

8. Enjoy!:

Share this delightful Indonesian snack with family and friends, especially during special occasions or as a delicious treat anytime.

Remember that "Panjang Mulud" is a versatile dish, and you can adjust the sweetness according to your preference. Enjoy your homemade "Panjang Mulud"!

350 cc of coconut milk

3 pieces of salam leaves (laurellike leaf used in cooking)
2 pieces of lime leaves
Salt

How to create:

Washed the bulled rice and drained, and then steamed with pandanus leaves until half-cooked during 20 - 30 minutes then lift and move to the bowl.Meanwhile, mixed the grated turmeric with 1 tbsp of coconut milk, and then filtered. Then Mix with coconut milk then entered the stem of Serai (Citronella), Salam leaves, lime leaves and salt. And cook using medium fire until boiled, while stirred to avoid break of coconut milk, continue cooking for 2 - 3 minutes, then lift from the fire.Pour piecemeal of coconut milk seasoned into the bowl which containing rice, stir well until evenly mixed till the coconut milk gone then move into the boiler and re-steam the rice until cooked.

3.) Make a frame. Place the shorter piece of bamboo on the fold of the paper, then tape it. The bamboo frame must be right at the corners of the paper.

29. tolong buatkan procedure text how to make nasi goreng. seperti contoh di atas:]​


Penjelasan:

How to Make Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice)

Ingredients:

- 2 cups of cooked and cold white rice

- 2 tablespoons of cooking oil

- 2 cloves of garlic, minced

- 2 shallots, thinly sliced

- 2 eggs, beaten

- 100 grams of chicken, shrimp, or tofu (optional)

- 2 tablespoons of sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)

- 1 tablespoon of regular soy sauce

- 1/2 teaspoon of salt

- 1/4 teaspoon of pepper

- Sliced cucumber, tomato, and lettuce for garnish (optional)

- Fried shallots for topping (optional)

Instructions:

1. **Prepare the Ingredients:**

- Ensure that the rice is cooked and cold. It's best to use leftover rice.

- If using chicken, shrimp, or tofu, cut them into small pieces.

2. **Heat the Oil:**

- Heat the cooking oil in a large frying pan or wok over medium-high heat.

3. **Sauté the Aromatics:**

- Add the minced garlic and sliced shallots to the hot oil.

- Sauté them until fragrant and the shallots become translucent.

4. **Cook the Protein (Optional):**

- If using chicken, shrimp, or tofu, add them to the pan and cook until they are fully cooked through.

5. **Scramble the Eggs:**

- Push the cooked ingredients to one side of the pan and pour the beaten eggs into the empty side.

- Scramble the eggs until they are fully cooked.

6. **Combine and Stir-Fry:**

- Mix the scrambled eggs with the rest of the ingredients in the pan.

7. **Add the Rice:**

- Add the cold rice to the pan. Break up any clumps and mix everything together.

8. **Season with Sauces:**

- Pour in the sweet soy sauce and regular soy sauce. Stir to evenly coat the rice and ingredients.

9. **Season with Salt and Pepper:**

- Sprinkle salt and pepper over the mixture and continue to stir-fry until the rice is heated through and well combined with the sauces.

10. **Garnish and Serve:**

- If desired, garnish with sliced cucumber, tomato, lettuce, and fried shallots.

- Serve the Nasi Goreng hot and enjoy!

Now you have a delicious plate of homemade Nasi Goreng. Selamat makan! (Enjoy your meal!)

How to Make Tumpeng

Tumpeng is a traditional Indonesian dish often served as a centerpiece during special occasions. It's a cone-shaped rice dish usually accompanied by various side dishes. Here's a step-by-step procedure on how to make tumpeng:

Ingredients:

2 cups of jasmine rice (or any preferred rice)

2 cups of coconut milk

1 pandan leaf (optional, for flavor)

Salt to taste

Banana leaves (for lining the cone shape)

Assorted side dishes (commonly include fried chicken, vegetables, eggs, and sambal)

Equipment:

Cone-shaped mold (traditionally made of bamboo, but you can use any cone-shaped object)

Steamer or rice cooker

Procedure:

1. Rinse the Rice:

Wash the rice thoroughly until the water runs clearto remove excess starch.

2. Cook the Rice:

Use a rice cooker or a saucepan to cook the rice. If using a saucepan, combine the rice, coconut milk, pandan leaf (for flavor), and a pinch of salt.

Cook the rice until it's soft and has absorbed the coconut milk. Remove the pandan leaf if used.

3. Prepare the Cone Mold:

Take a large banana leaf and cut it into a square shape.

Roll the square into a cone shape, ensuring that one end is narrower to create a cone.

Secure the shape with toothpicks or string if necessary.

4. Line the Cone with Banana Leaves:

Line the inside of the cone mold with fresh banana leaves. Make sure to overlap the leaves to prevent the rice from sticking to the mold.

5. Fill the Mold with Rice:

Scoop the cooked rice into the banana leaf-lined cone mold. Press the rice gently to ensure it takes the cone's shape.

6. Compact and Shape the Rice:

Use a spoon or your hand to compact the rice tightly inside the mold.

Shape it into a pointed cone. You can wet your hands to prevent the rice from sticking.

7. Remove the Mold:

Carefully remove the cone mold by sliding it upwards.

8. Decorate and Serve:

Decorate the tumpeng with various side dishes such as fried chicken, vegetables, hard-boiled eggs, and sambal. Arrange them around the base of the rice cone.

You can also garnish the tumpeng with sliced cucumbers, tomatoes, and krupuk (traditional Indonesian crackers).

Tumpeng is traditionally meant to be shared with family and friends. To serve, cut the tumpeng into portions and distribute it to your guests, starting with the honored guest.

9. Enjoy : )

Enjoy your homemade tumpeng as a delicious and visually appealing Indonesian dish, perfect for special occasions and celebrations.

Remember that tumpeng is not just about the taste; it also symbolizes unity and gratitude, making it a significant dish in Indonesian culture.
